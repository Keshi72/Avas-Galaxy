GLOWY DAY · MAINTAIN
Your skin is doing the work. Cleanse gently, tone, moisturize lightly. Don't over-treat. This is the baseline you're building toward every day — note what you did yesterday that got you here.
DRY & DULL DAY · FLOOD WITH WATER
Apply toner in two layers today — pat the first layer in, wait 30 seconds, pat a second layer. Follow immediately with moisturizer while skin is still damp. Your skin is thirsty. Give it water before anything else. Skip any actives today.
OILY DAY · BALANCE NOT STRIP
Counterintuitively, oily skin still needs moisture — stripping it makes it produce more oil. Cleanse properly today, use toner once, apply moisturizer very lightly. The gel moisturizer option is better on oily days than the lotion.

Check labels for: glucosamine, chitosan, and heavy marine-derived ingredients like carrageenan. Patch test any product containing algae or seaweed extracts on your inner arm for 24 hours before applying to your face. All products recommended here are safe.

SHOWER ROUTINE · DAILY

Designed around your sensory needs — minimal hand-to-skin product contact, satisfying clean feeling, fast absorption. The brush does most of the work so your hands don't have to.

Exfoliate with silicone brush
Long handle reaches your back completely. Firm bristles on the body, soft side for sensitive areas. Use in circular motions with your body wash. The satisfying deep clean that loofahs never deliver. Do this 3-4 times a week, not every day — over-exfoliating strips the skin barrier.
Cleanse with Sol de Janeiro Body Wash
Apply via the brush rather than your hands — the brush distributes it and the scent does the sensory work for you. Warm sweet gourmand, cardamom amber family. Lathers well enough to feel genuinely clean. Rinse thoroughly.
Moisturize immediately after — while still damp

HAND ROUTINE · AFTER HEALING

The goal is breaking the stripping cycle — every wash strips the barrier, the eczema worsens, the sensation makes you scrub harder, repeat. The routine below interrupts that cycle without requiring you to sit with uncomfortable sensations for long.

Switch to sulfate-free gentle soap
Vanicream Gentle Wash or CeraVe Hydrating Cleanser. No sulfates, no fragrance, glycerin-based. Doesn't lather as dramatically but cleans just as effectively without stripping. Use this every wash going forward.
Cold or lukewarm water only
You're already doing this right — keep it. Hot water is one of the biggest eczema triggers. Never hot, always cool to lukewarm.
Pat dry · don't scrub
The scrubbing dry is removing what little barrier is left after washing. Pat gently with a clean towel. The drying sensation will reduce over time as the barrier rebuilds — it feels urgent right now because the skin is compromised.
Apply CeraVe Healing Ointment immediately · while damp
Thin layer only. The damp skin means it absorbs in under a minute — shortening the uncomfortable application window as much as possible. This is the step that actually rebuilds the barrier over time. Consistency matters more than amount.
Overnight intensive · once fully healed
A slightly more generous layer of CeraVe Healing Ointment before sleep — no gloves until hands are completely healed and infection-free for at least two weeks. Let the ointment absorb naturally overnight. The repair happens while you sleep.
SEE A DERMATOLOGIST IF POSSIBLE
Eczema that progressed to infection is past the over-the-counter maintenance stage. A dermatologist can prescribe a short course of topical steroids or a barrier repair prescription that will get the inflammation under control properly and faster than OTC products alone. Worth pursuing once you have the means — this is a medical issue, not just a skincare one.
