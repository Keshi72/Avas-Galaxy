Tricep Dips
10 reps · use a sturdy chair. Hands grip edge, feet flat on floor, lower yourself by bending elbows to 90°. Targets the back of the arms directly.

Glute Bridges
15 reps · lie on back, feet flat and hip-width. Drive hips up, squeeze glutes at the top. Hold 2 seconds. Lower slowly. One of the most effective exercises for toning thighs and glutes.
GLUTES & THIGHS
✦
Sumo Squats
12 reps · feet wider than hips, toes slightly out. Lower until thighs are parallel. Drive through heels. Targets inner thighs specifically — one of your goal areas.
THIGHS & GLUTES
✦
Dead Bug
8 reps per side · lie on back, arms pointing up, knees at 90° above hips. Lower opposite arm and leg toward floor without arching your back. Slow and controlled. The best deep core exercise for a flat stomach.
DEEP CORE
✦
Bird Dog
10 reps per side · on all fours, spine neutral. Extend opposite arm and leg, hold 2 seconds, return. Creates a long, stable core — the foundation for the "toned" look rather than just strong.
CORE & BALANCE
✦
Lateral Leg Raises
12 reps per side · lie on your side, lift top leg to hip height and lower slowly. Targets outer thigh and hip. Add inner-thigh raises (bottom leg) for the inner thigh specifically.
THIGHS — OUTER & INNER

Plank Hold
20–30 seconds (build to 45s) · forearms on floor, body in a straight line. Don't hold your breath — breathe steadily. If full plank is too hard start with a knee plank and progress.

POST-WORKOUT WINDOW Eat protein within 30 minutes of finishing — Greek yoghurt, a boiled egg, or a banana with peanut butter. This is when your muscles absorb it best.
THE ONE RULE
CHANGE CLOTHES BEFORE YOU SIT DOWN
The moment work ends at 5pm — change into workout clothes before you do anything else. Before sitting down, before checking your phone, before eating. Sitting down first in day clothes is where the workout intention dies every single time. Clothes on = workout happening.
WEDNESDAY · 20 MINUTES · FLEXIBILITY & RESET

Wednesday flow is the most important session of your week for how you feel day-to-day. Sitting at a desk compresses your spine and tightens your hip flexors — this 20 minutes undoes the damage and resets your nervous system. No mat needed. Just your floor.

Child's Pose
Kneel, sit back toward heels, arms extended forward, forehead on the floor. Breathe deeply — feel your lower back expand. Decompresses the spine after sitting.
90 SECONDS · 3 SLOW BREATHS
Cat-Cow
On all fours — inhale and arch your back (cow), exhale and round your spine (cat). Slow and fluid. Restores mobility to every vertebra in your spine. Feel each segment move.
8 ROUNDS · BREATH-LED
Low Lunge Hip Flexor Stretch
One knee on floor, other foot forward. Sink your hips forward and down. Hold. Switch sides. This one stretch counters the damage of an entire week of sitting. The hip flexors are what cause low back pain, poor posture, and low energy.
60 SECONDS EACH SIDE
Seated Forward Fold
Sit with legs extended, hinge from the hips and reach toward your feet. Don't force it — go to your edge and breathe. Stretches the entire back of the legs and decompresses the lower spine.
90 SECONDS
Supine Twist
Lie on your back, draw one knee to chest and let it fall across your body. Arms wide. Look away from the knee. Wrings out tension from the lower back and spine. A deeply satisfying release.
60 SECONDS EACH SIDE
Figure-Four Glute Stretch
Lie on back, cross one ankle over the opposite knee, flex the foot. Draw both legs toward you. Targets the piriformis — the deep glute muscle that gets tight from sitting and contributes to back pain.
60 SECONDS EACH SIDE
Shoulder & Neck Release
Seated or lying. Gently drop one ear toward shoulder, hold. Roll neck slowly. Interlace hands behind head and gently nod forward. WFH tension lives here — release it before it becomes a headache.
2 MINUTES · SLOW
Legs Up the Wall
Lie with your sitting bones against the wall, legs resting straight up. Close your eyes. This is your Wednesday cool-down. Reduces leg swelling, calms the nervous system, reduces cortisol. Stay as long as feels good.
3–5 MINUTES
WEDNESDAY INTENTION
Wednesday flow is not a workout. It's maintenance and nervous system reset. You don't need to sweat. You don't need to feel like you "did something." The goal is to end the session feeling longer, looser, and calmer than when you started.
SATURDAY · 30 MINUTES · OUTSIDE

Your Saturday walk is the most underrated part of this plan. Walking outside regulates cortisol, improves mood, aids digestion, supports your skin, and builds baseline cardiovascular fitness without stressing your body. It also gets you out of the apartment — important when you WFH alone all week.

Before you leave
Drink a glass of water. Put on a playlist, podcast, or just let yourself have silence. Wear comfortable shoes — bare feet or grippy socks are fine indoors but your feet need support outside. Bring water if it's warm.
The walk itself
No pace goal. No heart rate goal. Walk at whatever speed feels natural and sustainable for 30 minutes. You can stop and look at things. That's allowed. The goal is 30 minutes outside, not 30 minutes of exercise.
Notice beautiful things
This is the AuDHD-specific intention for your walk. Notice textures, light, plants, architecture — whatever catches your eye. It engages the exploratory part of your brain that WFH can starve and it genuinely makes the walk feel easier and more restorative.
When you get back
Drink water. If you're in Austin, you've just done your neighbourhood exploring too. Note any streets or spots you want to come back to. Your Saturday walk will slowly become a way of knowing your city.
WHY OUTSIDE SPECIFICALLY
Outdoor walking in natural light suppresses cortisol and raises serotonin in a way that indoor walking doesn't. For AuDHD and delayed sleep phase specifically — morning or afternoon outdoor light helps regulate your body clock. Even on overcast days. The light through cloud is still 10x stronger than indoor lighting.
PROGRESS YOUR WALK OVER TIME
Month 1–2 · 30 minutes, any pace

Month 5–6 · try a 45-minute walk once a month
When you're in Austin · explore a new neighbourhood each Saturday

REST GUIDE
BETWEEN EXERCISES
30–45 seconds. Long enough to catch your breath, short enough to keep your heart rate up and the session within 25 minutes.
BETWEEN ROUNDS
60–90 seconds. Your muscles need slightly more recovery between full rounds. Use this time to drink water and shake out your limbs.
IF YOU NEED MORE
Take it. The plan is a guide not a rule. A workout where you rested longer and finished is better than one you abandoned because you pushed too hard.
