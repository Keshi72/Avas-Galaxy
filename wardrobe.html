YOUR SHAPE · PETITE HOURGLASS

Under 5'2" · defined waist · balanced chest and hips · your waist is your best feature. Every rule below serves one goal: show the waist, elongate the leg, and keep proportion balanced.

WEAR
  • Smocked & shirred bodices
  • Wrap dresses & skirts
  • Empire waist cuts
  • V-necklines
  • Midi & ankle lengths
  • Puff & bishop sleeves
  • Belted cardigans
  • High-waist skirts
  • Monochromatic dressing
  • Vertical prints & florals
AVOID
  • Boxy, shapeless cuts
  • Wide horizontal stripes
  • Drop-waist dresses
  • Maxi lengths with low waist
  • Heavy top + heavy bottom
  • Oversized everything at once
  • Cropped jackets (shortens)
  • Stiff structured fabrics
SILHOUETTE RULES · PETITE HEIGHT
SHOE COLOUR = HEM COLOUR
Match your shoe colour to your hem or as close as possible. This creates an unbroken line and visually adds 2–3 inches to your height. Nude shoes under a cream dress. Tan sandals under linen.
SIZE UP, ALTER THE WAIST
Always size to your largest measurement (usually chest or hips). Take a waist alteration — ~$15 and it transforms the fit entirely. Budget this into any purchase you love.
Note: smocked bodices and wrap styles self-adjust — no tailoring needed.
THE SLIP TRICK
A $10 slip underneath any dress prevents fabric riding up on thighs and clinging to skin. Solves the hem twist problem. Keeps everything falling correctly all day.
MIDI IS YOUR SWEET SPOT
Midi (below knee, above ankle) is both your most flattering length and your most on-aesthetic length. It covers your thighs while you're working toward your goals, hits cottagecore perfectly, and creates the vertical line that adds height.
PUFF SLEEVES WORK FOR YOU
Volume at the shoulder draws the eye upward and creates the illusion of height. For petite frames, puff sleeves are a friend — not a foe. Pair with a fitted bodice so the waist reads clearly.
ONE STATEMENT, ONE NEUTRAL
If the dress is the statement, keep shoes and bag neutral. If the bag is the statement, let the dress be simpler. Never more than one statement at once — for a petite frame it competes with your silhouette.
OUTFIT FORMULA · DAILY DRESSING
1
Choose a dress or skirt that shows or defines your waist Smocked, wrap, or empire waist. This is your anchor piece.
2

AUDHD SHOPPING RULES
THE PARKING LOT RULE
When you see something you want — screenshot it and save to your notes app under "Wardrobe Parking Lot." Do not buy. Review the folder once a month on your designated shopping day. Most of it won't feel urgent by then. The few that still feel right — those are the ones worth buying.
THE 48-HOUR RULE
If you've found something you want to buy — close the tab. If you're still thinking about it in 48 hours, it's genuinely worth buying. Hyperfocus wants it now. Your future self wants you to wait 48 hours.
BROWSE IN SHORT SESSIONS
Set a 20-minute timer before opening Depop. When it goes off — close the app. No open-ended scrolling. This prevents the spiral that ends in buying three things you don't need or buying nothing because you're overwhelmed.
ASK THREE QUESTIONS BEFORE BUYING
1. Is the fabric natural? (Check the label in the photos)
2. Does it show or define my waist?
3. Would I wear this in the next 30 days?
If any answer is no — parking lot, not cart.
